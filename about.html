Our Story

  • [Source]

    The Junior Reserve Officer's Training Corps (JROTC), a federal program sponsored by the United States Armed Services, is created as part of the National Defense Act. In accordance with Title 10, Section 2031 of the United States Code, the purpose of JROTC is declared to "instill in students in [United States] secondary educational institutions the values of citizenship, service to the United States, and personal responsibility and a sense of accomplishment." And thus exists to help high school students in developing character, leadership qualities, to strengthen patriotism and citizenship, and to encourage teamwork using basic military principles. The Boca Ciega High School JROTC program is currently sponsored by the United States Army.

  • JROTC comes to BCHS. However, it had a low student capacity (There were only two small companies by 1990), and was not run by a permanent instructor until the early 90's. An instructor from a different school would come and teach.

  • Lieutenant Colonel Wayne McGlamry, a former US Army artilleryman, becomes Senior Army Instructor (SAI) for the program. The students that made up most of the small program were rowdy and brutish. However, with a permanent instructor in place to remain focused on BCHS and teach the students, LTC McGlamry was able to help develop our JROTC program to the massive class size it is now (JROTC is currently at 300+ students, meaning it comprises nearly a quarter of the school population). LTC McGlamry is to retire during the 2014-2015 school year, after 22 years of service for the program, and for the Boca Ciega High School.

  • 1st Sergeant Michael Braccio, former US Army aviator and recruiter, is hired as an army instructor for the BCHS JROTC program.

  • Sergeant Major Freddy Whitehead, Jr., former US Army Ranger, is hired as an army instructor for the BCHS JROTC program.

  • Colonel Portugue is to fill in for LTC McGlamry's position as SAI for the upcoming 2014-2015 school year.
